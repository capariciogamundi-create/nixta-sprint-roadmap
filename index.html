What Nixta is | the thread that ties everything together

Nixta "Taqueria," built in real time by Rico Suave & Bigi Smalls, and the team behind it. Two proud creators - one Mexican-American, one Iranian-American. Edgar commands the kitchen. Sara runs the world. Between them: strong lineage, a fantastic palate, and a taqueria that made TIME100.

When Winter Storm Uri hit Austin in 2021 and thousands were without food or heat, Nixta and the Taco Mafia didn't wait - they cooked 3,000 free meals and showed up for their neighbors. The community fridge out front carries the tagline "Solidarity, Not Charity."

This is what the brand is actually built on. The design system we're creating needs to hold all of that - the silly and the serious, the ancestral and the unhinged.

P02 · W2 Identity — Nixta "Taqueria" Jul 7–11 ▾

We establish the Nixta "Taqueria" identity system that everything else plugs into - it's the connective tissue.

▶Voice + Tone

Nixta already has a voice - warm, funny, irreverent, deeply rooted in the culture it comes from and the community it belongs to. Seven years in Austin, Texas is no small thing.

Now we document it so it holds across a menu, an IG post, a press release, or a new hire writing emails. The freeze, the fridge, the Taco Mafia, the mutual aid - that thread lives here too. That's the soul of it.

James Beard. MICHELIN. Obama, Jay-Z, Beyoncé, Anne Hathaway. The State Department's Culinary Corps. Showing up for the neighborhood and for the world.

▶Color

Lock the official Nixta palette - defined, named, documented - and establish how it travels into each room.

▶Typography

Define the type system - what's universal, what's specific to each room, and how hierarchy works across every touchpoint from headings to captions.

▶Logo Rules

A few lockups exist, and so does the Corn Man! The rules we define here determine what, how, when, and where each one shows up.

▶First Visual Direction

An early look - not polished, but pointed. Gut check before we go deep. Full imagery direction lives in Phase 3.

Output: voice and tone guide · color palette · type system · logo usage guide

P03 · W3 The Rooms Jul 14–18 ▾

The house foundation has been established. Now we build room by room - distinct enough to stand alone, connected enough that you know exactly which house they came from. The sub-brands get their visual identity and every other room gets clarity on how it operates and connects.

Sub-brand Rooms

▶Xakali

"Small shelter." same physical space as Nixta, different energy, different culinary intention. Visual direction that holds its own tension - distinct spatial habitat, but unmistakably part of the house.

▶Xolovino

Has it's own look and feel - distinct enough to travel, scale, collab and carry merch, while always pointing back to the house's wavy-fun side.

▶Friendlys

Its own look and feel - intentional storytelling, travels, scales, can carry merch, while always pointing back to the house.

Rooms

▶Community / Movement

Not a sub-brand - a way of operating. A way of living the brand's ethos. The freeze, the fridge, the Taco Mafia, the mutual aid. And every activation and future expression of this spirit lives here.

▶Publishing / IP

From Tacos to Tahdig (October 6, Penguin Random House) is not directly merch nor a collab - it's a cultural artifact. That could become merch and collabs. This room holds the first book, the NASA 2027 project, and anything Nixta puts into the world as a permanent cultural object, virtually or physically.

▶"Guest Room"

Brand partnerships, guest chefs, outside creative. The framework for how Nixta shows up inside someone else's world - and how guests show up in Nixta's - without the house losing itself.

Systems

▶Signage direction

How the brand lives in physical space - exterior and interior. Part of the spatial architecture, thoughtful, not precious and not an afterthought.

▶Menu system + imagery direction

Menus are one of Nixta's highest-traffic brand touchpoints. We build a template system - fast to update, consistent in feel, unmistakably from the house no matter which room it's serving. Imagery direction covers illustration, photography style, and visual mood across all platforms.

▶Merch + Collabs

The system that runs across the house. We define what a Nixta drop looks like, how a collab is framed, and how any room can extend into something physical, digital, or collaborative without losing the house.

Output: sub-brand one-pagers · room definitions · menu template system · imagery direction · signage direction
